SOCIAL-JUSTICE DATA-SCIENCE

POSTDOCTORAL SCHOLAR AT UCLA<

Social justice, a general term that covers the concept of fair and just relations between individuals and society, manifests in the distribution of income, wealth, health, respect, freedoms, and opportunities. These issues are central to public policy, health, and society, as well as to the mission of UCLA. This is why the Division of Physical Sciences seeks to hire a postdoctoral scholar to work on a social-justice issue from a data-science perspective. The appointment will be as an Assistant Adjunct Professor either within the Department of Mathematics or the Department of Statistics.

By harnessing data, Data Science can accelerate the scientific study of social-justice issues and the Black experience. First, the recent advances in data science and the excellence both in UCLA Mathematics and Statistics in data science can be leveraged on research into the Black experience and social justice. Second, and just as importantly, anchoring data-science research in substantive social-justice issues will accelerate the reduction in bias and error of data-science methods.
